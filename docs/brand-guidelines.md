## 1. Brand Story & Mission

### Who We Are
Digital Implant is Azerbaijan's first and only clinic dedicated exclusively to **digital implantology**. Founded by Dr. Bakhtiyar Aliyev — a 3Shape-certified specialist and Straumann partner — the clinic operates from Babək Plaza, Baku, serving patients across Azerbaijan and the South Caucasus region.

### The Problem We Solve
In Azerbaijan, dental implant patients have historically faced two unacceptable choices: (1) traditional implantology with manual, skill-dependent placement and unpredictable outcomes, or (2) traveling abroad for higher standards at significant cost and inconvenience. Digital Implant exists to eliminate both choices by bringing European-standard digital precision to Baku.

### Why We Exist
Every patient deserves to know — before surgery — exactly where their implant will be placed, how it will look, and how it will function. Digital planning makes this possible. Our mission is to make that certainty the standard, not the exception, in Azerbaijani dentistry.

### Mission Statement
> To deliver European-standard dental implantology through digital precision — making every implant virtually planned, predictably executed, and permanently successful.

### Brand Promise
**Before we place a single implant, you see the outcome.**

## 2. Brand Values

### 1. Precision (Dəqiqlik / Точность)
Every decision is data-driven. We operate at 0.1mm accuracy because human health demands it. Precision means never improvising in the operating room — the plan is complete before the patient arrives for surgery.

*In practice:* We publish our 0.1mm accuracy claim; we explain how CBCT + 3Shape guides achieve it; we never round up or exaggerate clinical claims.

### 2. Transparency (Şəffaflıq / Прозрачность)
Patients see their virtual plan before surgery. Pricing is clear. Processes are explained. We do not hide complexity behind clinical jargon or withhold information that patients need to make confident decisions.

*In practice:* We show the digital workflow step-by-step; we explain what CBCT is and why it matters; we share before/after imaging with patient consent.

### 3. Innovation (İnnovasiya / Инновация)
We adopt technologies that measurably improve patient outcomes, not trends for their own sake. 3Shape Implant Studio, cone beam CT, and static surgical guides are used because they produce better results — and we explain the evidence.

*In practice:* We name specific technologies; we explain what each does; we describe how they improve outcomes versus traditional methods.

### 4. Expertise (Ekspertlik / Экспертиза)
Dr. Bakhtiyar Aliyev has performed 3,100+ implant procedures over 15+ years — exclusively in implantology. This depth of focus is our credibility. We do not claim to be a general dentistry practice; we are specialists.

*In practice:* We lead with credentials (3Shape certified, Straumann partner, European training); we use case volume and years to build trust; we never over-claim beyond the specialty.

### 5. Care (Qayğı / Забота)
Technology does not replace humanity. Every patient receives a personalized consultation and a plan made specifically for their anatomy. We treat anxiety as a clinical factor and design the experience to reduce it.

*In practice:* We address patient fears in copy; we emphasize the "before you decide" phase; appointment CTAs are warm and accessible, not clinical-cold.

## 5. Brand Voice & Tone

### Personality Traits (Brand Adjectives)
1. **Precise** — we are specific, never vague; we use numbers and technical terms where they add credibility
2. **Confident** — we state facts clearly; no hedging or apologetic language
3. **Modern** — forward-looking, technology-forward; we embrace digital vocabulary
4. **Human** — warm without being casual; we speak to people, not at patients
5. **Authoritative** — we are the expert; we educate, not just persuade

### Tone by Context

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Educational content (blog, FAQ)** | Calm, expert, informative | "CBCT imaging allows us to visualize bone density at 0.1mm resolution before placing a single guide." |
| **Homepage / hero copy** | Bold, direct, aspirational | "Digital Precision. Perfect Outcomes." |
| **Appointment / CTA** | Warm, accessible, non-pressuring | "Book a consultation — see your plan before you decide." |
| **Doctor bio** | Confident, personal, credentialed | "Every planning session is personally conducted by Dr. Aliyev." |
| **Error / technical** | Calm, helpful, solution-oriented | "Something went wrong. Try again or call us directly." |
| **Post-procedure follow-up** | Caring, reassuring, celebratory | "Your implant is in. Here's what to expect in the next 72 hours." |

### Brand Sounds Like
- A precise, trustworthy specialist explaining your options clearly
- A modern European clinic, not a Soviet-era health system
- A technology company that understands medicine (think: clinical + digital)

### Brand Does NOT Sound Like
- Aggressive sales ("Call now! Limited spots!")
- Overly clinical jargon without explanation
- Generic medical marketing ("caring for your smile since…")
- Casual/friendly to the point of minimizing clinical seriousness
- Overpromising ("guaranteed perfect results")

## 7. Visual Identity Rules

### Logo Usage

**Primary logo:** "Digital Implant" wordmark (preferred rendering)

**Clear Space:** Minimum clear space equals the cap-height of the "D" in "Digital" on all four sides. Never crowd the logo with other elements.

**Minimum Size:** No smaller than 120px wide on digital; no smaller than 30mm on print.

**Approved backgrounds:**
- Deep navy (#020B18, #050E1F, #071628) — primary
- White (#FFFFFF) — print/document use only

**Prohibited modifications:**
- Do not change logo colors outside the approved palette
- Do not apply gradients to the logo wordmark itself
- Do not add drop shadows
- Do not stretch or distort aspect ratio
- Do not place on photographic backgrounds without a dark overlay (#020B18 at 80%+ opacity)
- Do not use on light-colored backgrounds in primary brand contexts

### Typography

| Role | Font | Weight | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Display / Headings** | Space Grotesk | 700 (Bold), 600 (Semibold) | All H1–H3, CTAs, stats, eyebrow labels |
| **Body / UI** | Inter | 400 (Regular) | All body text, descriptions, form labels |
| **Fallback** | system-ui, sans-serif | — | Safe fallback stack |

**Typography hierarchy:**
- H1: Space Grotesk Bold, `clamp(2.4rem, 5vw, 4rem)`, line-height 1.05
- H2: Space Grotesk Bold, `2rem–2.5rem`, line-height 1.2
- H3: Space Grotesk Semibold, `1rem–1.125rem`, line-height 1.3
- Body: Inter Regular, `1rem`, line-height 1.6–1.7
- Eyebrow/Label: Space Grotesk Semibold, `0.625rem` (10px), letter-spacing `0.4em`, ALL CAPS
- Caption/Stat label: `0.75rem` (12px), letter-spacing wide, uppercase

**Rules:**
- Never use font weights below 400 for body
- Labels/eyebrows are always uppercase with wide tracking
- Body copy is never uppercase
- Mixed-language copy (AZ/EN/RU) uses the same type hierarchy

## 8. Photography & Imagery Style

### Approved Visual Styles

**Clinical precision imagery:**
- 3D renderings and digital planning screenshots from 3Shape software
- CBCT scan visualizations
- Surgical guide/template close-ups (clean, well-lit, on white or dark surfaces)
- Implant anatomical diagrams (the SVG diagram in Hero is the reference style)

**Clinic environment:**
- Clean, modern operatory — dark/white surfaces, precision instruments visible
- Doctor in professional attire (scrubs/lab coat), focused, performing precision work
- No clutter, no visible outdated equipment

**Patient imagery:**
- Natural, confident smiles — not exaggerated or overly posed
- Before/after clinical photos: standardized framing, neutral background, consistent lighting
- Diverse demographic representation of Azerbaijani/CIS patients (ages 35–60 emphasis)

**Technology/abstract:**
- Data visualization, orbit rings, grid backgrounds — the design system's blue-on-dark aesthetic
- Subtle glow effects on technical imagery are on-brand

### Prohibited Image Types
- Stock-photo "happy dentist with patient" clichés (generic dental stock)
- Extreme clinical imagery (blood, open surgical sites) in public-facing content
- Low-resolution imagery (minimum 1920px wide for hero; 1200px for cards)
- Images with competitor logos or unrecognized implant system branding
- Overly saturated lifestyle photography that looks disconnected from the clinic's precision aesthetic
- Cheesy "thumbs up" or overly staged emotional patient photography

## 9. Language & Copy Rules

### General Writing Style
- **Register:** Formal-professional, not casual; clinical-expert, not academic-dry
- **Person:** First person plural for the clinic ("we deliver"), second person singular for the patient ("you see")
- **Sentence length:** Short to medium. Precision language is punchy. Avoid run-on sentences.
- **Tone marker:** When in doubt, ask: "Does this sound like a trusted specialist who respects the patient's intelligence?"

### Azerbaijani (Primary Language)
- Use formal literary Azerbaijani — no dialectal or colloquial shorthand
- Procedure names in Azerbaijani first, with English technical term in parentheses where helpful: *"rehberli cərrahiyyə (guided surgery)"*
- Medical terms may appear in English/Latin if no established Azerbaijani equivalent exists; italicize if introducing a term
- AZ tone: slightly more formal than English counterpart; respect-based register ("Siz" not "sən" in all patient-facing copy)

### Russian (Secondary Language)
- Standard Russian; no regional dialect
- Slightly warmer and more explanatory than AZ content — Russian-speaking visitors may be less familiar with the clinic
- Procedure names in Russian with technical English term where helpful
- Use "Вы" (formal you) always — never informal "ты" in patient-facing copy
- RU tone: slightly warmer, more narrative than AZ; Russian audiences respond well to slightly more explanation

### English (Tertiary — Reference/SEO)
- Clean, international professional English
- American spelling conventions
- Use for meta descriptions, international SEO, developer-facing documentation

| Implant planning | İmplant Planlaşdırması | Планирование Импланта | Implant Planning |

### Punctuation & Formatting Preferences
- Em dashes (—) for editorial separators, not hyphens
- Ellipses only in patient quotes, never in brand copy
- Bullet points: use for credentials, feature lists, procedural steps — not for prose
- Numbers: always use numerals for statistics ("3,100+", "0.1mm", "15+"), not words
- Decimal: use period (0.1mm) in English/AZ; comma (0,1 мм) in Russian
- Brand name in copy: "Digital Implant" — never "digital implant" (lowercase) or "digitalimplant.az" as a name

## 10. Brand Applications

### Website (digitalimplant.az)
- Dark theme is mandatory — #020B18 base background
- Glass cards with backdrop-filter blur for content panels
- Blue grid background at 20–60% opacity for visual texture
- All CTAs use Primary Blue (#0057FF) with glow-shadow effect
- Section labels use Sky (#00B4FF) color, uppercase, Space Grotesk, wide tracking
- No light-mode variant without explicit design system change

### Social Media (Instagram / LinkedIn)
- Dark background consistent with website palette
- Avoid white/light background posts — maintain brand visual consistency
- Use text gradients sparingly (hero callout words only)
- Clinical imagery: consistent framing, clinic aesthetic
- Story/Reel CTAs: "Link in bio" → appointment page
- Hashtag strategy (AZ primary, RU secondary): #digitalimplant, #implantbaku, #implantasiya, #bakudentist, #implantstudio

### Email Communications
- Subject lines: clear, specific ("Randevunuz təsdiqləndi — 27 İyun, 14:00")
- Header: white logo on dark (#020B18) banner
- Body: white/off-white on dark background OR white background with dark text for maximum deliverability
- Footer: address, phone, unsubscribe

### Print Materials (Brochures, Consent Forms, Clinical Documents)
- White background acceptable for print materials where dark printing is impractical
- Logo: on white backgrounds, use the full dark-color version (not reversed)
- Typography: Space Grotesk headings, Inter body; maintain hierarchy
- Clinical/consent documents: formal header with clinic name and logo, consistent font hierarchy

### Medical Documents (Patient Records, Plans, Reports)
- Header: "Digital Implant — Dr. Bakhtiyar Aliyev" + clinic address
- Language: match patient's preferred language (AZ/RU)
- Technical documents: precise terminology, no brand voice softening — clarity over personality
- 3Shape planning reports: export with clinic letterhead when shared with patients

## 11. Brand Dos and Don'ts

| # | Do ✓ | Don't ✗ |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Use "Digital Implant" as the brand name (capitalized) | Write "digital implant" lowercase or "DigitalImplant" as one word |
| 2 | Lead with the doctor's name and credentials when building trust | Omit Dr. Aliyev's identity in favor of generic clinic branding |
| 3 | State statistics precisely: "3,100+ implants", "0.1mm" | Round numbers loosely or say "thousands of implants" without citing |
| 4 | Name specific technologies: "3Shape Implant Studio", "CBCT" | Say "advanced technology" without specifying what or why |
| 5 | Use the approved dark color palette on all digital touchpoints | Create light-theme variations without Design System approval |
| 6 | Write Azerbaijani procedure names formally with capital first letter | Write Az copy in informal/colloquial register |
| 7 | Use formal "Siz/Вы" in all patient-facing copy | Use informal "sən/ты" even in "friendly" contexts |
| 8 | Publish clinical claims after Medical Content Manager review | Publish medical statistics or outcomes without medical review |
| 9 | Use Space Grotesk for all headings and CTAs | Substitute other display fonts |
| 10 | Show the digital workflow end-to-end in educational content | Skip the "why digital" explanation and just list services |
| 11 | Show before/after cases with standardized photography | Use inconsistent lighting, angles, or framing in clinical photos |
| 12 | Link to the appointment page as the primary conversion action | Provide only a phone number with no digital booking path |
| 13 | Describe the guided surgery protocol step-by-step | Use vague phrases like "precise surgery" without explanation |
| 14 | Credit implant brands by name (Straumann, NeoBiotech, etc.) | Use unnamed "premium brands" language |
| 15 | Treat patient anxiety as real — acknowledge fear in copy | Dismiss concerns with "it's simple/painless" without qualifying |
| 16 | Use em dashes for editorial separation | Use hyphens as em dashes or overuse ellipsis |
| 17 | Keep English terminology in parentheses when introducing AZ terms | Write English-only copy in AZ-primary pages |
| 18 | Apply blue CTA glow shadow for primary buttons | Apply glow effects to secondary/ghost buttons |
| 19 | Use glass-morphism cards with blur for content panels | Use flat opaque light cards in brand contexts |
| 20 | Mark social content with #digitalimplant consistently | Post untagged content or use only generic dental hashtags |
